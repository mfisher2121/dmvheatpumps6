## How the Cost-Shifting Scam Works

### The Infrastructure Problem

Data centers need:
* Massive electrical substations
* High-voltage transmission lines
* Redundant grid connections (99.999% uptime requirement)
* Backup power systems

**Example: Single 100 MW data center campus requires:**
* New 230 kV transmission line: $50-100 million
* Substation upgrades: $30-60 million
* Distribution system reinforcement: $20-40 million
* **Total infrastructure: $100-200 million**

**Question: Who pays for this infrastructure?**

**Answer: You do. Through "infrastructure surcharges" on your electric bill.**

### The Regulatory Sleight of Hand

Here's how it works in practice:

**Step 1: Tech company negotiates with utility**
* "We want to build a 150 MW data center"
* "We'll be your largest customer, billions in revenue"
* "But we need guaranteed power and redundancy"

**Step 2: Utility proposes infrastructure upgrades**
* Submits plan to state utility commission
* "We need to upgrade substations, transmission lines, etc."
* "Cost: $200 million"

**Step 3: Utility commission approves (they always do)**
* Justification: "Economic development"
* "Will create jobs, tax revenue, attract more business"
* Commissioners often have ties to utility industry (regulatory capture)

**Step 4: Utility builds infrastructure**
* Borrows $200 million
* Guaranteed rate of return: 9-11% annually (set by commission)
* That's $18-22 million profit per year on this project alone

**Step 5: Utility adds cost to "rate base"**
* All customers pay proportionally
* Residential customers: 40-50% of total cost
* Commercial: 30-40%
* Industrial/data centers: 10-20%

**The math:**
* Infrastructure cost: $200 million
* Residential share: $80-100 million
* Spread across 500,000 residential customers
* Your share: $160-200 added to your bill permanently
* Plus 9-11% annual return to utility
* Your annual cost: $14-22/year forever

**One data center = $14-22/year added to your bill.**

Dominion/Pepco are building infrastructure for 50+ data centers.

**Do the math.**
